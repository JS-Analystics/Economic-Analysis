The presentation summarizes the important economic takeaways from the Russian Attack on Ukraine

The further diagrams show the predictions of an economic shock of input goods for production.
This is modelled through a decrease in Total Factor Productivity z beginning by a downward shift of the production curve under New Keynesian Assumptions.
In the long term it is assumed that the input goods can be substituted which leads to an upwards shift of the production curve, yet not to the original level.
Besides that it is assumed that the reduction in input goods leads to less labor demand which causes the Shift on the curve itself.
All other shifts on the diagrams are results from these two implications due to a shift in Production.
This can be assumed to be true because the Price Increases in Gas and Oil due to the War in Ukraine and due to the War in Iran cause higher production cost
and therefore lower Output due to less efficient productivity which is to be considered equal to a decrease in Total Factor Productivity.

The difference in reality and prediction can be explained by the ECB aiming for an Inflation of 2% which has automatic implications for the Interest Rate Target
and the increase / decrease of Money Supply.
Also, the New Keynesian Model implements the Assumption of Sticky Prices. This is not to be applicable to the entire economy, Gas Prices for example are very flexible.
However, large parts of the economy, especially those that are dependent on contracts, cannot immediately adjust prices.
Therefore, many times it can be observed that numbers are stagnating instead of decreasing.
